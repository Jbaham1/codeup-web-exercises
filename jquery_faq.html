  1. Who is the director of the National Park Service?
    2. The National Park Service is a bureau of the Department of the Interior. Directly overseeing its operation is the department's Assistant Secretary for Fish, Wildlife and Parks.
  2. How many employees are in the National Park Service?
    • Permanent, temporary, and seasonal employees: Approximately 20,000
    • Volunteers: More than 279,000 in 2019
  3. What is the largest national park site? Smallest?
    • Largest: Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve, AK, at 13.2 million acres
    • Smallest: Thaddeus Kosciuszko National Memorial, PA, at 0.02 acres
  4. How many people visit the national parks?
    Total recreation visitors to the national parks in 2019: 327,516,619.
  5. What is the most-visited national park?
    Golden Gate National Recreation Area
  6. What are concessions?
    Concessioners provide park visitors with lodging, transportation, food services, shops, guiding, and other services. More than 480 NPS concession contracts in more than 100 different park units vary in size from small, family-owned businesses to national/international corporations.
  7. Can I bring my pet to a national park?
    Some national parks welcome pets—in developed areas, on many trails and campgrounds, and in some lodging facilities.
  8. What do I need to know about driving off road in national parks?
    Before you head out, check with the national parks that you intend to visit. In many national parks, off-road driving is illegal.
  9. How old is the National Park System?
    The National Park Service was created by an act signed by President Woodrow Wilson on August 25, 1916. Yellowstone National Park was established by an act signed by President Ulysses S. Grant on March 1, 1872, as the nation's first national park.
  10. What government agency oversees the National Park Service?
    The National Park Service is a bureau of the Department of the Interior. Directly overseeing its operation is the department's Assistant Secretary for Fish, Wildlife and Parks.

  • Hot Springs

    • Hot Springs National Park covers a total area of 5,550 acres
    • Hot Springs was made a national park on March 4, 1921
    • The lowest elevation found in Hot Springs is 415 feet at Bull Bayou
    • There is no entrance fee for Hot Springs National Park

  • Channel Islands

    • Painted Cave and the other sea caves on Santa Cruz Island were the inspiration for some of the sets on Pirates of the Caribbean.
    • “Peter Pan” (released in 1924) was filmed on Santa Cruz Island, portions at, well, Tinker’s Cove.
    • The oldest human remains in North America, dating to 13,000 B.C., were discovered in 1959 on Santa Rosa Island
    • Diablo Peak (Devil’s Peak), on Santa Cruz Island, is the tallest peak on the Channel Islands at 2,450 feet.

  • Redwood

    • Redwood National Park covers a total area of 131,983 acres
    • Redwood was made a national park on October 2, 1968
    • The lowest elevation found in Redwood is 0 feet at the Pacific Ocean
    • The highest elevation found in Redwood is 3,170 feet on Coyote Peak
